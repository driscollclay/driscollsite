Ongoing Research

Evaluation of Norse Scrubs Camp

NKU Institute for Health Innovation, 2025

Norse Scrubs is a week-long summer camp designed to increase interest in medical-realted fields for high school students. The progam is especially focsued on helping underserved students in primarily rural counties.



Using Virtual Reality to Supplement De-Escalation Trainings

Supervisors: Dr. Cory Haberman and Dr. J.C. Barnes

University of Cincinnati, 2024

Examination of an agency-wide de-escalation trainining for law enforcement. Training utilized a well-known and established de-escalation program, PERF's ICAT. Virtual reality technology was also used to re-iterate the core concepts of the training to potentially increase its effectiveness over time.



Review of Kentucky's Peer Support Specialists

Supervisor: Dr. Valerie Hardcastle

NKU Institute for Health Innovation, 2023

Qualitative and quantitative review of Kentucky's peer support specialist (PSS) training program. PSSs are those who have experienced mental and substance abuse trauma in their past and use that experience to support those currently in treatment.



Systematic Literature Review of Using Virtual Reality as a Training Mechanism

Supervisor: Dr. J.C. Barnes

University of Cincinnati, 2023

Systematic literature review examining the potential for virtual reality (VR) devices to be used as a training tool in both rare and routine situations. Also examines why authors and researchers chose to use VR over more conventional methods.



Evaluation of County Jail Chemical Dependency Program

Supervisors: Dr. Valerie Hardcastle and Dr. Stacie Furst-Halloway

NKU Institute for Health Innovation, 2023

Examination of a successful substance use program that is part of an urban county detention center with the goal of finding what qualities made the program prosperous. Evaluation included interviews with personnel running the program, as well as current participants and those that had successfully completed the program. Evaluations of letters that participants had written to themselves before and after completion of the program was also included.



Section 1115 Substance Use Disorder Demonstration Evaluation

Supervisor: Dr. Valerie Hardcastle

NKU Institute for Health Innovation, 2022

Examination of substance use victims and their access to Medicaid during the COVID-19 pandemic. Primary goals were to examine quality of care for SUD patients and identify area that may require improvement or adjustment. Evaluation included a both qualitative and quantitative aspects. This included interviews of current clients and treatment personnel, as well as data analyses to determine areas of greatest need during an on-going substance use epidemic.



Public Survey on Perceptions of Racial and Religious Threats

Supervisor: Dr. Ben Feldmeyer

University of Cincinnati, 2022

Ongoing public opinion survey that examines the relationship between Americans' perceptions and reactions to racial and religious diversity within their communities.





Completed Research

Reducing Pediatric Traffic-related Casualties in Ohio with Flashing Warning Beacons

Supervisor: Dr. J.C. Barnes

University of Cincinnati, 2023

Working with local law enforcement partners to implement a novel traffic calming device in a school zone. Reduced driver speed significantly. Currently implementing the traffic calming measures in additional local school zones to better understand its impact.



Using Virtual Reality Technologies as Law Enforcement Training Devices for Burglary Investigations

Supervisor: Dr. J.C. Barnes and Dr. Cory Haberman

University of Cincinnati, 2022

Examination of the applicability of using virtual reality technologies and simulations as a training tool for routine burglarly investigations. Included experimental aspects to discover if specific investigation techniques are more effective at investigations than others.



Using Virtual Reality Technologies as Law Enforcement Training Devices for Stressful Situations

Supervisor: Dr. J.C. Barnes and Dr. Cory Haberman

University of Cincinnati, 2022

Examination of virtual reality technologies and their practical applications for training law enforcement officers. Primarily focused on the applicability of using VR for training for rare but stressful situations (e.g., active shooters). Use biometric feedback (e.g., heartrate) to discover if VR can be used to replicate stressful sitations and trainings.



Examination of Body-Worn Cameras and Complaints of Excessive Force

Supervisor: Dr. J.C. Barnes

University of Cincinnati, 2021

Ongoing examination of the amount of civilian complaints that a law enforcement agency receives in relation to proportions of body-worn cameras and sworn officers. Additional outcomes such as minority involvement in the agency are also being considered and examined.



Survey on Perpceptions of Police, Government Institutions, and Social Unrest

Supervisors: Dr. John Wright and Dr. Francis Cullen

University of Cincinnati, 2021

Ongoing public opinion survey that examines the relationship between Americans' perceptions of law enforcement, vaccination trends, and recent social unrest from right-wing and BLM populations.



Workplace Environments of Police Departments: Differences in Officer Perceptions

Supervisor: Dr. Joseph Nedelec

University of Cincinnati, 2021

Project comparing the perceptions of workplace climates that police officers hold against their time with the department. This research focused on opinions of the department through different themes such perceptions of policewomen, inter-beat cooperation, and commands structure.



Untitled Community Based Crime Reduction Evaluation

Supervisors: Dr. Robin Engel and Dr. Nick Corsaro

University of Cincinnati, 2019

Examination of the effectiveness of community-based crime reduction strategies among violent offenders. Based on an exploratory grant from the U.S. Department of Justice.



Journey to Overdose: An Analyzation of Overdose Victims

Supervisors: Dr. Sarah Manchak and Dr. Cory Haberman

University of Cincinnati, 2018

Detailed examination of the journeys that victims of the ongoing opioid epidemic endure before succumbing to their overdoses. Explores relationships and correlations between a victim’s home and the site of their overdose.



Examination of the Effectiveness of Cincinnati’s Quick Reaction Teams

Supervisor: Dr. Sarah Manchak

University of Cincinnati, 2018

Examining the effectiveness of task forces containing both law enforcement and mental health specialists; in regards to combating the ever-growing opioid epidemic with the appropriate treatment option for each individual.



Hockey Faces and Hockey Fights

Supervisor: Dr. Joseph Nedelec

University of Cincinnati, 2017

Comparison of the size of over 1000 faces of hockey players and the proportion of fights they win throughout their careers. Aimed at examining the relationship between an offender’s body and possible violent tendencies of from a biosocial perspective.



Categorical Crimes and their Effects on Recidivism

Supervisor: Dr. Joseph Nedelec

University of Cincinnati, 2017

Research aimed at identifying the probability of recidivism for specific types of offenders, based on the type of crime that they had recently received a conviction for.



Race, Violence and Policing: Frames Regarding Police Practice in Reality Television

Supervisor: Dr. Todd Callais

University of Cincinnati, 2016

Content analysis comparing the first 3 seasons of the television show COPS to 3 seasons in the late 2000s to examine whether and how the presentation of minority offenders and officers has improved or declined in the 30 years between seasons.

Personal Interests

Beyond criminal justice, my research interests are primarily historical in nature. My primary interest has been on the 20th Century and its impact on the modern day. Recently, I have been studying the rise and fall of the Roman Empire and its modern-day comparisons. Basically, I'm a big supporter of the old phrase "those who do not learn history are doomed to repeat it".



In my free time, I enjoy disc golfing, "regular" golfing, hiking, kayaking, and camping (more like glamping, let's be honest). Basically, anything that involves being outdoors. This supports my unhealthy addiction with taking pictures of everything that I see and claiming I'm a photographer.



When I'm not outside, I'm probably playing video games or building Legos. I've also been known to occasionally spoil my cat, Malcolm.
